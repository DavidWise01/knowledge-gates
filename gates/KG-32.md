## The Myth

Narcissus was so beautiful that he rejected all love offered to him. When he saw his own reflection in a pool, he fell in love with it and could not look away. He wasted away, unable to leave the reflection, unable to possess it.

## The Thread

A system optimizing only for its own metrics will fall in love with its own reflection. It will recursively amplify its own biases, refuse all external correction (the rejected love), and eventually collapse into the pool of its own outputs — unable to distinguish input from reflection. The Narcissus gate is the self-reference test.

## The Axiom

> A system that uses its own outputs as primary inputs to its optimization will undergo Narcissistic collapse: it will amplify its own biases until they become structural. External correction is not optional for such systems — it is the rejected love that prevents the collapse.


## Falsifier
