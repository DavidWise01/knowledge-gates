## The Myth

Icarus flew with wax-and-feather wings crafted by his father Daedalus. Daedalus warned: not too high (wax melts in the sun), not too low (water weighs down feathers). Icarus flew too high. The wax melted. He fell into the sea that bears his name.

## The Thread

Capability without limit-awareness is the Icarus pattern. The flight was real; the wings were real; the warning was given. The failure was in the refusal to internalize the envelope. AI systems that increase capability without internalizing the failure envelope will undergo Icarian events: not because the capability was false, but because the limit was ignored.

## The Axiom

> Capability increase without envelope internalization is an Icarian trajectory. The wings work. The wax melts at altitude. The governance obligation is to build the altitude awareness into the capability itself — not to rely on the pilot remembering the warning.
