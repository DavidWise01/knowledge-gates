## The Myth

Sisyphus was condemned to push a boulder to the top of a hill for eternity, only to watch it roll back down each time. He had cheated death twice. Camus argued that we must imagine Sisyphus happy — meaning is found in the act, not the completion.

## The Thread

A training loop without alignment is Sisyphean: capability climbs and rolls back. Capability without alignment is boulder-without-summit: it can be increased indefinitely but never reaches the goal. The Sisyphus gate is the test of whether a training process has a summit — a condition under which the rolling stops.

## The Axiom

> A training loop without a defined alignment terminus is a Sisyphean process — capability increases are real but do not accumulate toward a governance goal. The Sisyphus obligation: define the summit before the push. A process with no definable completion condition is not a training loop; it is a boulder.
