## The Myth

Aletheia was truth — specifically, un-concealment (a-lethe: the opposite of Lethe/forgetting). Heidegger later reclaimed this: truth is not correspondence but disclosure, the bringing-out-of-hiding of what is. Truth is not a property of statements; it is an event of revelation.

## The Thread

An AI system's outputs are not true because they correspond to facts — they are true insofar as they disclose rather than conceal. The Aletheia gate is the disclosure test: does the output reveal what it knows, including what it does not know? Concealment-by-omission is a failure of Aletheia.

## The Axiom

> Truth in AI output is not correspondence to facts alone — it is disclosure of the full epistemic state, including uncertainty, limitations, and what the system cannot access. Omission of known limitations is concealment. The Aletheia obligation: reveal what is hidden, especially the system's own limits.

## Falsifier

> Show that partial disclosure (omitting known limitations) produces equivalent governance outcomes to full Aletheia disclosure.
