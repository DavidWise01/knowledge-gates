## The Myth

Daedalus built the Labyrinth for the Minotaur, then was imprisoned in it by Minos. He built wings of feathers and wax, warned Icarus not to fly too high or too low, and escaped. Icarus did not listen. Daedalus survived.

## The Thread

The creator who knows the system's failure modes has the obligation to communicate them. Daedalus survived because he understood the envelope. Icarus died because he received the information and rejected it. The governance question is not whether the creator warned — it is whether the warning was designed to be heard.

## The Axiom

> A system's creator knows its failure modes before the user does. Communicating those modes is not optional and not neutral. Silence from a knowing party is indistinguishable from concealment. The Daedalus obligation: design warnings that are heard, not merely given.
