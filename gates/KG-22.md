## The Myth

The Pythia spoke Apollo's words while in trance at the omphalos — the navel of the world. The temple bore two commands: γνῶθι σεαυτόν (Know thyself) and μηδὲν ἄγαν (Nothing in excess). The Oracle's answers were always true but rarely literal.

## The Thread

The Oracle knows; the questioner interprets. When the interpretation fails, the questioner blames the Oracle. But the Oracle gave a true answer — the governance failure was in the interface between truth and interpretation. AI systems give true answers to the questions asked, not the questions intended. The Delphic gate is the interpretation interface.

## The Axiom

> An AI system answers the question it received, not the question intended. The gap between received and intended question is the Delphic gap — always present, often consequential. The governance obligation is to minimize the gap, not to pretend it does not exist.
